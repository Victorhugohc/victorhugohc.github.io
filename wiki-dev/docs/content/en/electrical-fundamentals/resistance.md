# Resistance

## Overview

Resistance is the opposition to the flow of electrical current in a conductor or component. It determines how easily current can pass through a material.

It is measured in ohms (Ω) and is a fundamental property of all electrical circuits.

## Purpose

Resistance exists to:

- Control the amount of current in a circuit
- Regulate energy consumption in electrical devices
- Protect components from excessive current
- Enable proper operation of electrical and electronic systems

All real conductors have some level of resistance, even if very low.

## How It Works

When current flows through a material:

- Electrons collide with atoms inside the conductor
- These collisions oppose the movement of charge
- This opposition is resistance

The level of resistance depends on several factors:

- Material type (e.g., copper vs aluminum)
- Length of the conductor
- Cross-sectional area
- Temperature

Higher resistance reduces current flow for a given voltage, while lower resistance allows more current to pass.

Resistance also causes energy loss in the form of heat.

## Applications

Resistance plays a role in all electrical systems, including:

- Electrical wiring and cable systems
- Heating elements and resistive loads
- Electronic circuits and components
- Motor windings and transformers
- Protection and control devices

It is both a useful property and a limitation, depending on the application.

## Key Considerations

- **Material Selection:** Conductors with low resistance improve efficiency
- **Heat Generation:** High resistance leads to energy loss and temperature rise
- **Cable Length:** Longer cables increase total resistance
- **Cross Section:** Larger conductors reduce resistance
- **Temperature Effects:** Resistance increases with temperature in most conductors
- **Energy Losses:** Resistance contributes to voltage drop and reduced system efficiency

Ignoring resistance effects can result in overheating, voltage drop, and poor performance.

## Practical Notes

- Copper is widely used due to its low resistance and good conductivity
- Resistance is a key factor in voltage drop over long cable runs
- Connections and terminals can introduce additional resistance if not properly installed
- Excessive resistance at a connection point is a common cause of localized overheating
