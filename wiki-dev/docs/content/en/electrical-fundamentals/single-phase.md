# Single-Phase

## Overview

Single-phase power is an AC electrical system that uses one alternating voltage waveform. It is the simplest and most common form of power distribution for low-power applications.

It typically consists of two conductors: a phase (live) and a neutral.

## Purpose

Single-phase systems are used to:

- Supply power to low and moderate loads
- Simplify electrical distribution in smaller systems
- Provide power for residential and light commercial equipment
- Support control circuits and auxiliary systems

They offer a simple and cost-effective solution where high power is not required.

## How It Works

In a single-phase system:

- A sinusoidal voltage is supplied between the phase and neutral conductors
- The current flows in one alternating waveform
- Power is delivered in pulses, rising and falling with the waveform

Because the power fluctuates over time, it is not as constant as in multi-phase systems.

## Applications

Single-phase power is commonly used in:

- Residential installations
- Lighting systems
- Small motors and appliances
- Control panels and auxiliary circuits
- Office and commercial equipment

It is widely used where loads are relatively small and evenly distributed.

## Key Considerations

- **Power Capacity:** Limited compared to three-phase systems
- **Efficiency:** Less efficient for high-power applications
- **Motor Performance:** Single-phase motors are less efficient and have lower starting torque
- **Voltage Levels:** Common levels include 120V, 127V, 220V, or 230V depending on the region
- **Load Balance:** Not applicable in the same way as multi-phase systems, but uneven loading can still affect supply quality
- **Wiring Simplicity:** Requires fewer conductors and simpler installation

Using single-phase for high-power loads can lead to inefficiency and unstable operation.

## Practical Notes

- Commonly used for control circuits even in large industrial systems
- Often derived from a three-phase system for convenience
- Single-phase motors may require capacitors for starting
- Suitable for small loads but not ideal for heavy industrial equipment
