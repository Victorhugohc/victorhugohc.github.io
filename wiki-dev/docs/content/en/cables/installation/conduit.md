# Conduit

## Overview

Conduit is a protective system used to route and safeguard electrical cables in industrial installations. It consists of tubes or channels that enclose cables, providing mechanical protection and environmental isolation.

Conduit is widely used in industrial environments where cables are exposed to physical damage, harsh conditions, or require organized routing.

---

## Purpose

Conduit systems are used to:

- Protect cables from mechanical damage  
- Shield cables from environmental conditions (moisture, dust, chemicals)  
- Provide organized and structured cable routing  
- Improve safety by enclosing live conductors  
- Facilitate maintenance and future cable replacement  

---

## How It Works

Cables are installed inside conduit pathways, which act as a physical barrier between the cable and its surroundings.

The conduit:

- Prevents direct exposure to external elements  
- Contains and organizes cable runs  
- Limits mechanical stress on cables  

However, enclosing cables also affects heat dissipation, which must be considered during cable sizing.

---

## Common Types of Conduit

### Rigid Metal Conduit (RMC)

- Made of steel or aluminum  
- High mechanical strength  
- Suitable for harsh environments  

**Applications:**

- Heavy industrial installations  
- Areas with high risk of impact  

---

### Electrical Metallic Tubing (EMT)

- Thin-wall metal conduit  
- Lighter and easier to install than RMC  

**Applications:**

- Indoor installations  
- Commercial and light industrial environments  

---

### PVC Conduit

- Non-metallic and corrosion-resistant  
- Lightweight and cost-effective  

**Applications:**

- Underground installations  
- Corrosive environments  

---

### Flexible Conduit

- Made of flexible metal or plastic  
- Allows movement and vibration  

**Applications:**

- Connections to motors and moving equipment  
- Areas requiring flexibility  

---

## Applications

Conduit is used in:

- Industrial facilities and plants  
- Control panel wiring (external routing)  
- Outdoor and underground installations  
- Hazardous or corrosive environments  

---

## Key Considerations

When selecting conduit:

- **Mechanical protection:** required level of durability  
- **Environmental conditions:** moisture, chemicals, temperature  
- **Flexibility needs:** fixed vs moving installations  
- **Corrosion resistance:** especially in harsh environments  
- **Installation complexity:** ease of routing and assembly  
- **Heat dissipation:** enclosed cables may require derating  

---

## Practical Notes

- Conduit improves protection but reduces heat dissipation, which affects cable ampacity.
- Overfilling conduit makes installation difficult and can cause overheating.
- Proper sealing is important in outdoor or hazardous environments.
- Flexible conduit should be used where vibration or movement is present.
- Conduit systems should be planned to allow future expansion and maintenance.
