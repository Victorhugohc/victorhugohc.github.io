# Machine Safety Standards

## Overview

Machine safety standards define the requirements for designing machines that minimize risk to operators, maintenance personnel, and nearby equipment.

They establish principles and criteria for identifying hazards, implementing protective measures, and ensuring that safety functions perform reliably.

## Purpose

Machine safety standards are used to:

- Reduce the risk of injury and accidents  
- Provide a structured approach to hazard identification and risk reduction  
- Define requirements for safety-related control systems  
- Establish acceptable levels of risk in machine operation  
- Support regulatory compliance and machine approval  

They ensure that safety is systematically integrated into machine design.

## How It Works

Machine safety standards follow a risk-based methodology.

They define:

- Hazard identification and risk evaluation processes  
- Risk reduction strategies (inherently safe design, safeguarding, control measures)  
- Safety functions and their required reliability levels  
- Validation and verification requirements  

Risk reduction typically follows a hierarchy:

1. Eliminate hazards through design  
2. Apply protective measures (guards, interlocks)  
3. Implement control-based safety functions  

Safety-related control systems must meet defined performance levels or integrity levels, depending on the standard used.

## Applications

Machine safety standards apply to:

- Automated machinery and production lines  
- Robotic systems and cells  
- Packaging, processing, and assembly equipment  
- OEM machines and custom-built equipment  

They are critical in any system where human interaction with machinery presents potential hazards.

## Key Considerations

- Risk assessment determines required safety measures  
- Safety functions must be reliable under defined conditions  
- Different standards may define different evaluation methods  
- Human interaction and access points must be carefully analyzed  
- Safety design must consider the full machine lifecycle  

## Practical Notes

- Do not rely solely on control systems; combine mechanical and electrical protection  
- Clearly define each safety function and its purpose  
- Ensure proper integration between safety devices and control systems  
- Validate safety functions under real operating conditions  
- Poorly implemented safety systems can create a false sense of protection
