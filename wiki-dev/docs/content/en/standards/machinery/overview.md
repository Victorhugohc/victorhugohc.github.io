
# Machinery Standards Overview

## Overview

Machinery standards define the requirements for the safe design, construction, and operation of industrial machines.

They focus on reducing risks associated with mechanical movement, electrical hazards, and human interaction with equipment.

## Purpose

Machinery standards are used to:

- Ensure the safety of operators and maintenance personnel  
- Define a structured approach to risk reduction  
- Establish requirements for machine design and protective measures  
- Support regulatory compliance and machine approval  
- Reduce accidents and operational hazards  

They provide a framework for designing machines that are safe throughout their lifecycle.

## How It Works

Machinery standards are based on a risk-based approach.

They define:

- Methods to identify and evaluate hazards  
- Requirements for implementing protective measures  
- Safety functions and their required reliability  
- Design principles to minimize or eliminate risks  

The process typically involves:

- Identifying hazards associated with the machine  
- Evaluating the level of risk  
- Applying appropriate risk reduction measures  
- Verifying that safety requirements are met  

These standards do not define a single solution but establish the criteria that the solution must meet.

## Applications

Machinery standards apply to:

- Industrial machines and production equipment  
- Automated systems and robotic cells  
- Packaging, processing, and assembly equipment  
- OEM machinery and custom-built systems  

They are essential in any system where human interaction with moving or energized equipment is present.

## Key Considerations

- Safety must be considered from the early design stage  
- Risk assessment is a fundamental part of compliance  
- Protective measures may include mechanical, electrical, and control solutions  
- Requirements vary depending on machine type and region  
- Documentation is critical for validation and approval  

## Practical Notes

- Do not rely on a single safety measure; use layered protection  
- Design for safe operation, maintenance, and troubleshooting  
- Clearly define safety functions and their expected behavior  
- Consider foreseeable misuse, not only intended operation  
- Incomplete safety design can lead to serious hazards and legal issues
