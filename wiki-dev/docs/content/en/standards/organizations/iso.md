# ISO

## Overview

The International Organization for Standardization (ISO) develops international standards across a wide range of industries, including manufacturing, quality management, and machinery safety.

ISO standards focus on system-level requirements, processes, and methodologies rather than specific electrical or product construction details.

## Purpose

ISO standards are used to:

- Establish consistent processes and best practices  
- Improve safety, quality, and reliability of systems  
- Provide structured methodologies for engineering and design  
- Support international alignment across industries  
- Enable organizations to demonstrate standardized practices  

They are widely used to define how systems should be designed, evaluated, and managed.

## How It Works

ISO standards define:

- General principles and frameworks  
- Methodologies for analysis and decision-making  
- Requirements for processes and system performance  
- Guidelines for documentation and validation  

They are typically applied at a system or organizational level rather than at a specific component level.

In many cases, ISO standards are used together with other standards to provide a complete solution.

## Applications

ISO standards apply to:

- Machinery design and safety methodologies  
- Risk assessment processes  
- Quality management systems  
- Manufacturing and operational practices  
- Documentation and process control  

They are commonly used in:

- Industrial machine design  
- OEM equipment development  
- Organizations seeking standardized processes  
- International projects requiring consistent methodologies  

## Key Considerations

- ISO standards often define frameworks rather than specific technical solutions  
- They are frequently used in combination with other standards  
- Compliance may require documented processes and traceability  
- Some ISO standards are widely adopted even when not mandatory  
- Understanding the scope of each ISO standard is critical  

## Practical Notes

- Use ISO standards to guide methodology, not just compliance  
- Ensure proper documentation to demonstrate adherence  
- Combine ISO standards with application-specific standards when required  
- Do not assume ISO standards replace technical equipment standards  
- Strong alignment with ISO practices improves consistency and audit readiness
