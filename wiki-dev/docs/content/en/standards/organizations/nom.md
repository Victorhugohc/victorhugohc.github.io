# NOM

## Overview

Normas Oficiales Mexicanas (NOM) are mandatory standards issued by the Mexican government that define requirements for safety, performance, labeling, and installation of products and systems.

They establish the legal framework for compliance in Mexico and are enforced by regulatory authorities.

## Purpose

NOM standards are used to:

- Ensure safety of people and infrastructure  
- Define mandatory technical and installation requirements  
- Regulate products entering and used within Mexico  
- Support inspection, approval, and legal compliance  
- Align national requirements with international practices when applicable  

They are required for equipment and systems to be legally installed and operated in Mexico.

## How It Works

NOM standards define:

- Technical requirements and performance criteria  
- Safety rules and protection measures  
- Testing and verification procedures  
- Labeling and documentation requirements  

Compliance is typically verified through:

- Accredited testing laboratories  
- Certification bodies authorized in Mexico  
- Official markings and documentation  

Unlike voluntary standards, NOM compliance is mandatory for applicable products and installations.

## Applications

NOM standards apply to:

- Electrical equipment and installations  
- Control panels and industrial systems  
- Consumer and industrial products entering the Mexican market  
- Machinery and safety-related systems  

They are required in:

- Industrial facilities in Mexico  
- Imported equipment and machinery  
- Projects subject to inspection by local authorities  

## Key Considerations

- NOM standards are legally mandatory, not optional  
- Certification is often required for product approval and importation  
- NOM standards may reference or align with international standards  
- Documentation and labeling must meet specific national requirements  
- Non-compliance can result in rejection, fines, or operational restrictions  

## Practical Notes

- Always verify which NOM standards apply to the project or equipment  
- Ensure certification is completed before installation or import  
- Do not assume international compliance automatically satisfies NOM requirements  
- Work with certified laboratories or approved bodies when required  
- Missing NOM compliance is a common cause of project delays in Mexico
