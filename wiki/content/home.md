# Automation Handbook

**Automation Handbook** is an open knowledge resource dedicated to **industrial automation, electrical engineering, and control systems**.

The goal of this project is to provide **clear, structured, and practical explanations** of the technologies used in modern industrial systems — from basic electrical concepts to PLC programming, industrial networks, motion control, and troubleshooting.

Unlike scattered documentation or vendor-specific manuals, this handbook focuses on **fundamental concepts that apply across different manufacturers and industries**.

Whether you are a **student, technician, engineer, or automation enthusiast**, this handbook aims to become a **practical reference for learning and solving real-world automation problems**.

---

# Why This Handbook Exists

Industrial automation knowledge is often:

- Fragmented across vendor manuals  
- Hidden behind paywalls or expensive training  
- Overly academic without practical application  
- Poorly explained for beginners  

This project was created to build a **structured and practical reference** that bridges the gap between theory and real industrial systems.

The handbook focuses on:

- Clear explanations  
- Practical understanding  
- Real industrial context  
- Vendor-neutral knowledge  

---

# What You Will Find Here

The handbook is organized into sections that follow the **logical structure of an industrial automation system**.

## Electrical Fundamentals

Understand the core principles that power every automation system.

Topics include:

- Voltage, Current, and Resistance  
- Ohm’s Law  
- AC vs DC Systems  
- Single-Phase and Three-Phase Power  
- Power Factor

- Ethernet/IP  
- Modbus RTU

- Profibus  
- CAN Bus

You will learn **how devices communicate and how industrial networks are structured**.
