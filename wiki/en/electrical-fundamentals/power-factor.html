Power Factor ​

Overview ​

Power factor is a measure of how effectively electrical power is being used in an AC system. It represents the relationship between the useful power (active power) and the total power supplied (apparent power).

It is expressed as a value between 0 and 1.

Purpose ​

Power factor is used to:

  • Evaluate how efficiently electrical power is utilized
  • Identify losses caused by reactive components
  • Optimize system performance and capacity
  • Reduce energy costs and penalties
  • Improve overall power quality

A higher power factor indicates more efficient use of electrical power.

How It Works ​

In AC systems, not all supplied power is converted into useful work.

Power can be divided into:

  • Active Power (kW): performs useful work
  • Reactive Power (kVAR): sustains magnetic and electric fields
  • Apparent Power (kVA): total power supplied

Power factor is defined as the ratio between active power and apparent power:

Power Factor=Active Power (kW)Apparent Power (kVA)

Inductive loads such as motors and transformers cause current to lag behind voltage, reducing power factor.

Applications ​

Power factor is relevant in:

  • Industrial motor systems
  • Power distribution networks
  • HVAC systems and compressors
  • Facilities with large inductive loads
  • Energy monitoring and management systems

It is a key parameter for efficient operation of electrical systems.

Key Considerations ​

  • Low Power Factor: Increases current, causing higher losses and heating
  • System Capacity: Poor power factor reduces available system capacity
  • Utility Penalties: Many utilities charge for low power factor
  • Correction Methods: Capacitor banks or active correction systems are used to improve it
  • Load Type: Inductive loads typically reduce power factor
  • Efficiency Impact: Improving power factor reduces energy losses

Ignoring power factor can lead to oversized equipment and higher operational costs.

Practical Notes ​

  • A power factor close to 1.0 is ideal
  • Typical industrial systems operate between 0.8 and 0.95
  • Capacitor banks are commonly used for correction
  • Monitoring power factor helps detect inefficient or overloaded systems
